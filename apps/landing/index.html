Quiz

The page starts asking you things

Prism pulls the claims out of what you're reading and turns them into questions you have to answer before moving on. Passive skimming stops being an option — which is the whole point.

On a WSJ piece on rate cuts → “What happens to bond prices when rates fall, and why?”
Story

Retold as something that happened to someone

Abstract mechanisms become a sequence of events with stakes. Narrative is the format human memory is built for — the concept arrives attached to something that moves.

Inflation → a household's grocery bill across eighteen months.
Game

Play the idea instead of reading it

The concept becomes something with a goal, a constraint, and immediate feedback on every move. Feedback lands on the action, not at the end of a session — no streak to protect, nothing to lose by stopping.

Diversification → allocate a portfolio, watch a sector crash, see what survived.
Simulate

Move the numbers yourself

Any figure on the page becomes a control you can drag. Predict first, then move it — the gap between what you expected and what happened is where the concept actually lands.

“7% annual return” → what 1% of fees costs you across thirty years.
Digest

The part that actually matters

A 3,000-word article carries maybe four load-bearing ideas. Prism finds them, shows what each one depends on, and gets out of the way.

Long explainer → four claims, the evidence for each, what's still contested.
Coach

Guided, one hint at a time

For a problem rather than an article. Prism finds the first thing that went wrong instead of saying “incorrect,” and the final answer stays locked until you've genuinely tried — enforced server-side, not by hiding a button.

Highlight a problem → attempt → precise hint → solve → prove it on a new one.
How

It reads the page you chose — and nothing else

Prism only ever looks at a page when you ask it to, shows you exactly what it's about to read, and never runs in the background. Minimum Chrome permissions, no browsing history, no silent monitoring.

Financial, health, email and payment pages are excluded from automatic reading by default.
